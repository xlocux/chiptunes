# 🧬 Neon Keygen Archive  
### Chiptunes from the Cracking Scene

> *Echoes from the modem era.*  
> *Bits, beeps, and neon dreams from forgotten trainers and keygens.*

---

## 📟 About

This repository is a **nostalgic archive of 8-bit chiptunes** extracted from classic **keygens, trainers, cracktros, and scene intros**.

Long before streaming platforms and massive game soundtracks, the **cracking scene** produced tiny executable artworks:  
a few kilobytes of code, a scrolling message, a glowing logo… and a hypnotic **chiptune looping in the background**.

These melodies were not just background noise.

They were the **soundtrack of digital rebellion**.

This repo preserves those sounds.

---

## 🕹 What You'll Find

Inside the archive you may encounter:

- **Keygen chiptunes**
- **Trainer menu music**
- **Cracktro soundtracks**
- **Scene demo loops**
- **Retro synth fragments**
- **FM / tracker era compositions**

Many were originally written using:

- FastTracker
- Impulse Tracker
- ProTracker
- custom tracker engines embedded in keygens

Expect formats like:

```
.mod
.xm
.it
.s3m
.wav
.mp3
```

Some files may also be **recordings extracted from executables** where the original module wasn't available.

## 🌆 A Note on the Scene

The cracking scene was more than piracy.

It was:

- a **technical playground**
- an **underground art movement**
- a **global network of coders, musicians, and graphic artists**

Keygens and trainers often included:

- scrolling greets
- ANSI art
- glitch visuals
- and unforgettable **chip music**

Many musicians from that era later became **professional game composers**.

## 💾 Archive Philosophy

This repository aims to:

- preserve **historic chiptunes**
- document **scene culture**
- celebrate **tracker music**

It does **not** distribute cracked software.

Only the **music and cultural artifacts** surrounding the scene.
